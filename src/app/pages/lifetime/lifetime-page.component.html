Every year, the London Chess Club awards Lifetime Achievement Awards to a small number of individuals who have shaped the club into what it is today. The LCC is honoured to recognize the remarkable achievements and contributions of all these individuals.


Their lifelong dedication to the sport of chess and their unwavering support for the London Chess Club have truly made a lasting impact.

Steve Killi has been a pillar of the London Chess Club for many years. He has nurtured and taught countless young players, helping them develop not only their chess skills but also their character and sportsmanship. He brought the club to its current location at the Kiwanis Senior Centre. His unwavering dedication to the club and the sport has left a mark on the chess community.

Jay Zendrowski was a passionate chess player and talented novelist. He was recognized as a National Master by the CFC and won numerous tournaments at the LCC, including 5-time Standard Chess Champion, 6-time Active Chess Champion, and 4-time Speed Chess Champion. At his peak, Jay was among the top 60 chess players in Canada. Jay shared his love of chess with players of all skill levels and mentored many up-and-coming players. Without his guiding hand, the London Chess Club would not be the same today.

David Jackson was a passionate and accomplished chess player. A fixture at the London Chess Club for many years, David held the title of National Master, and his brilliant gameplay was widely recognized with his games regularly featured in chess publications. He dominated the board, setting intricate traps and captivating opponents with his exciting and unpredictable style of play. Over his illustrious career, David amassed numerous championship titles and tournament victories in both classic and faster-paced chess formats. At the age of 14, David led the German Club Men's Team to a championship victory, and later that year, he was among the 25 players who faced off against the legendary World Champion, Boris Spassky. In addition to his exceptional playing abilities, David also served multiple terms as the President of the London Chess Club, further cementing his legacy within the local chess community.

Don Armstrong's long-standing association with the London Chess Club has been marked by his unwavering commitment and multifaceted contributions. As a dedicated president and volunteer, he has generously donated his time and resources to the club, ensuring its smooth operation and continued success. The London Chess Club is truly fortunate to have had Don Armstrong as part of its family for many years. His enduring legacy as a volunteer, coach, and leader will continue to positively impact the lives of chess players for years to come.
