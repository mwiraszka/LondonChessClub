The London Chess Club Lifetime Achievement Award, started in 2023, recognizes members who have made a significant difference to our community. Our club brings together chess players of all ages and skill levels, and these awards celebrate the individuals whose dedication has helped shape our welcoming space. Recipients are chosen for their long-term commitment to chess and the substantial contributions they've made to the club's growth and success.

Recipients have contributed to our community in various meaningful ways. Some are skilled competitors who have represented the LCC in tournaments, raising our club's profile. Others are dedicated teachers who have patiently helped beginners develop their skills. Many have served in leadership roles as club officers, tournament directors, and event organizers. What unites them all is their willingness to give their time and energy to support chess in London.

The impact of these members extends beyond the chessboard. They've secured playing venues, mentored younger players, and created the inclusive atmosphere that defines our club today. By recognizing these individuals, we acknowledge how their efforts have made chess more accessible and enjoyable for everyone. Their legacy continues to inspire current and future generations of chess enthusiasts in our community.

2024

Don Armstrong's long-standing association with the London Chess Club has been marked by his unwavering commitment and multifaceted contributions. As a dedicated president and volunteer, he has generously donated his time and resources to the club, ensuring its smooth operation and continued success. The London Chess Club is truly fortunate to have had Don Armstrong as part of its family for many years. His enduring legacy as a volunteer, coach, and leader will continue to positively impact the lives of chess players for years to come.

David Jackson was a passionate and accomplished chess player. A fixture at the London Chess Club for many years, David held the title of National Master, and his brilliant gameplay was widely recognized with his games regularly featured in chess publications. He dominated the board, setting intricate traps and captivating opponents with his exciting and unpredictable style of play.

Over his illustrious career, David amassed numerous championship titles and tournament victories in both classic and faster-paced chess formats. At the age of 14, David led the German Club Men's Team to a championship victory, and later that year, he was among the 25 players who faced off against the legendary World Champion, Boris Spassky. In addition to his exceptional playing abilities, David also served multiple terms as the President of the London Chess Club, further cementing his legacy within the local chess community.

Steve Killi has been a pillar of the London Chess Club for many years. He has nurtured and taught countless young players, helping them develop not only their chess skills but also their character and sportsmanship. He brought the club to its current location at the Kiwanis Senior Centre. His unwavering dedication to the club and the sport has left a mark on the chess community.

Jay Zendrowski was a passionate chess player and talented novelist. He was recognized as a National Master by the CFC and won numerous tournaments at the LCC, including 5-time Standard Chess Champion, 6-time Active Chess Champion, and 4-time Speed Chess Champion.

At his peak, Jay was among the top 60 chess players in Canada. Jay shared his love of chess with players of all skill levels and mentored many up-and-coming players. Without his guiding hand, the London Chess Club would not be the same today.
