Every fall, chess players from around London and the surrounding region gather to compete for the city's most coveted chess prize: the Championship Cup.

Peter Murray won the inaugural tournament in 1967, and defended his title the following year. Since then, the trophy has changed hands over 20 times, with 17 different players having been crowned London Chess Champion to date.

In 2019, Kevin Gibson (2296) defeated Steve Demmery (2230) to retain his title as London Champion. The city could not have asked for a better spectacle: the match was drawn after three rounds, meaning a win in round 4 could clinch the victory for either player. Kevin did just that, winning his 5th (!!) consecutive title, and 8th total.

The London Chess Championship was canceled in 2020 and 2021 due to the pandemic, but 2022 will more than make up for it. 50 players are currently participating in the event — more than ever before in its entire 55-year history!

Players are split into 3 sections by rating, with each section comprising of two groups. Section A and B groups play an 8-player round robin, whereas Section C groups have 9 players each, thus completing this phase of the tournament one week later.

Winners and runner-ups from each group will then play each other in a 4-game playoff to determine the winners for each section. The winner of Section B gets automatically bumped up to Section A next year. The winner of Section A is crowned the new London Chess Champion.
